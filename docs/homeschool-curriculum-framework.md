# SurvivalOS Homeschool Curriculum Framework

This framework document serves as the formal curriculum guide for **SurvivalOS Survival Academy**, detailing pedagogical alignment with standard U.S. and Canadian homeschooling benchmarks.

---

## 1. Pedagogical Philosophy

The Survival Academy utilizes a **Pragmatic-Constructivist** pedagogy. It connects standard educational disciplines (Mathematics, Sciences, Literacy, Technology) to practical, real-world survival applications. This hands-on context increases learner engagement, promotes critical thinking, and prepares children for independent off-grid operations.

### Key Tenets:
*   **Offline-First & Self-Paced:** Operates entirely locally. Designed for student-led navigation with minimal adult supervision.
*   **Interactive Feedback:** Quizzes provide instant validation, and interactive 3D flashcards promote spaced repetition.
*   **Integrated Scratchpad:** Whiteboard drawing canvas mimics scratch-paper calculation, reinforcing kinesthetic writing of equations.

---

## 2. Subject & Standards Mapping

### 📐 Mathematics Track
Mathematical training is mapped directly to **U.S. Common Core State Standards (CCSS)** and **Canadian Provincial Mathematics Curriculum Objectives** (such as the Ontario Mathematics Curriculum).

*   **Counting in Nature (Pre-K / Sprouts):**
    *   *Focus:* Visual quantity association, counting up to 10, basic subtraction.
    *   *U.S. Standards:* `CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.K.CC.B.4` (Understand the relationship between numbers and quantities).
    *   *Canadian Alignment:* Ontario Kindergarten Program (Demonstrate an understanding of number relationship).
*   **Campfire Math (Grade 3 / Explorers):**
    *   *Focus:* Multiplication, multi-step word problems, resource depletion math.
    *   *U.S. Standards:* `CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.3.OA.A.1` (Multiplication concepts), `3.NBT.A.2` (Addition/subtraction within 1000).
    *   *Canadian Alignment:* Grade 3 Number Sense (Solve problems involving multiplication and division of two-digit numbers).

### 🔬 Science & Engineering Track
Science courses map to the **Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)** and **Canadian Science Curriculum Guidelines** (e.g. BC Science Curricular Competencies).

*   **Safe Foraging Basics (Grade 4 / Explorers):**
    *   *Focus:* Local botany, plant classification, identification of toxicity indicators.
    *   *U.S. Standards:* `NextGen Science: 4-LS1-1` (Structure and function of plants).
    *   *Canadian Alignment:* Grade 4 Life Sciences (Compare structures and behaviors of local plants).
*   **Basic Electronics & Radio (Grade 9 / Cadets):**
    *   *Focus:* Ohm's Law, electronic component functions, electromagnetic spectrum.
    *   *U.S. Standards:* `NextGen Science: HS-PS3-3` (Design, build, and refine a device that works within given constraints).
    *   *Canadian Alignment:* Grade 9 Physics (Electricity & Circuitry).
*   **Off-Grid Solar Load Calculation (Vocational / Operators):**
    *   *Focus:* Voltage, current, amp-hour capacity calculations, solar array sizing.
    *   *U.S. Standards:* Aligned to standard vocational training guidelines.

## 3. Grading & Record Auditing

Student assessments are logged locally in a permanent report ledger (`localStorage`). 
*   **Quizzes:** Scored out of total questions. A score of **70% or higher** is required to pass a module.
*   **Flashcards:** Tracks "Mastered" vs. "Needs Review" cards. A deck is considered mastered once the student logs 100% "Got It!" marks.
*   **Audit Readiness:** Parents or state/provincial inspectors can open the **Student Report Card** panel to review a complete log of scores, dates, and export a CSV transcript as official homeschool completion proof.
